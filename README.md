Problem Statement

Delays in repairing public infrastructure such as potholes, malfunctioning streetlights, and illegal waste dumping are persistent issues in modern cities.
Current reporting methods often involve tedious manual forms, imprecise location descriptions, and slow human verification processes. Consequently, minor issues escalate into significant hazards (e.g., road accidents or hygiene crises) before authorities can intervene.
Furthermore, without precise GPS data and clear visual evidence, technical teams struggle to locate the reported incidents efficiently within a complex urban landscape. 
This inefficiency leads to wasted resources, increased maintenance costs, and a decline in the quality of urban life for residents.

Objective

CityGuard AI serves as an intelligent bridge between citizens and city authorities.
When a resident uploads a photo of a defect, AI Computer Vision instantly analyzes the image to verify the type of damage (e.g., distinguishing between a burst pipe and localized flooding).
The AI then generates an automated technical report, assessing the severity level (e.g., "High Risk" for deep potholes on main roads).
Integrated with automated GPS tracking, the system plots these reports onto a centralized digital map for city managers.
This allows authorities to optimize repair logistics based on AI-prioritized data, significantly reducing response times from days to hours and ensuring a data-driven approach to urban maintenance.
